The Town Seal

A seal the town holds together.

Generate the town key at a public meeting. Split it among named officers. Publish only the seal. No one person can sign alone, and no retirement can lose the town its name.

Every clerk asks the same question: what happens when I retire, or lose my laptop? A single private key is a single point of failure. The Town Seal answers it in the open. You generate the key once, in the room, split it into shares, and hand one to each officer. Any 3 of 5 can reconstruct the key when the town needs to sign. A lost share below that number is nothing. The whole key is never in one place again.

Any k of N shares reconstruct the town key. Choose k so no single officer can act alone, and no single loss can lock the town out. Default 3-of-5.

Do this on a screen the room can see, on a device you trust. The secret key exists in this page's memory for exactly one moment: while it is generated, split, and signed. It is never saved to this device, and never sent over any network. Print the officer cards before you leave the page. Reload and the whole key is gone, held only in the shares.

Officer share cards

Each officer gets one card, and only this once. Print them, hand them out, and do not keep a copy of anyone else's. Below-threshold loss is fine. Above-threshold gathering is the town signing, and a breach if it is not the town.

Publish the Town Seal

One signed declaration goes to the open network: the town adopts this public seal, and records signed by this key are the town's official record. It carries the officers' names and roles and the threshold, but never a share and never the secret. It is signed once with the key you just generated, then the key is discarded from memory.
